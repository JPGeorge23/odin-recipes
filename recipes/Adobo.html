Adobo

Description

It's been called the unofficial national dish of the Philippines. But ask anyone of Filipino heritage how to make their beloved chicken adobo, and everyone will give you a different answer. And they're all correct.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup white vinegar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 head of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon whole black peppercorns
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 4-6 boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 1/4 cup canola oil

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, white vinegar, brown sugar, garlic, peppercorns, and bay leaf.
  2. Add the chicken thighs to the marinade and toss to coat. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  3. In a large skillet or Dutch oven, heat the canola oil over medium-high heat. Remove the chicken from the marinade and shake off any excess. Reserve the marinade.
  4. Working in batches, add the chicken to the skillet or Dutch oven and brown on both sides, about 5 minutes per side.
  5. Return all of the chicken to the skillet or Dutch oven, add the reserved marinade and enough water to cover the chicken. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce has thickened, about 30 minutes.
  6. Remove the bay leaf and serve the chicken over rice, garnished with green onions or cilantro if desired.
